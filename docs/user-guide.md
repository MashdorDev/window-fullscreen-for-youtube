# User Guide

## What is windowed fullscreen?

It enlarges the YouTube video to fill your **browser window** — edge to edge — without
going into operating-system fullscreen. You keep your taskbar, tabs, and other windows
visible and clickable. It's the "cinema, but not full takeover" middle ground.

## Turning it on and off

- Click the **windowed-fullscreen button** added to the player's bottom-right controls
  (next to the native fullscreen button), or
- Press the **hotkey** (default `Shift+F`), or
- Press **`Esc`** to exit.

## Options

Open the options from the toolbar icon, or via YouTube's player **gear menu** (the most
common toggles appear there too). Settings sync across your signed-in browsers.

### Hotkey

Set any combination, e.g. `Shift+F`, `Ctrl+Alt+W`. Modifiers: `Shift`, `Ctrl`, `Alt`,
`Meta`. `Esc` always exits.

### Behavior

- **Auto-enter on new video** — automatically enters windowed fullscreen each time a new
  video starts.
- **Scrollable mode** — keeps the page scrollable while the player is enlarged, so you
  can scroll down to comments and related videos.
- **Sticky chat** — when on, live chat stays pinned to the right edge and the comments
  below respect its width. When off, chat sits in YouTube's normal column and scrolls
  with the page. You can drag the chat's left edge to resize it.

### Hide while active

Granular control over what disappears in windowed fullscreen:

- **Masthead (top bar)** — hidden by default; move the mouse to the very top to reveal it.
- **Sidebar (related videos)**
- **Comments**

Use **Reset to defaults** to restore everything.

## Notes

- Works on watch pages, live streams (`/live/`), and clips.
- The chat button only appears when a stream/premiere has live chat.
- Nothing leaves your device — see the Privacy Policy.
